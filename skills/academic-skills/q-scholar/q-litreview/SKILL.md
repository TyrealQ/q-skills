# Q-Litreview

This skill guides drafting and refinement of standalone literature review sections that follow the introduction. It produces flowing academic prose organized as a progressive argument across two subsections, arriving at research questions as the earned conclusion of the theoretical and empirical case.

## Reference Files

- **references/literature_review_template.md**: Structural guidance (not verbatim scripts); draft dynamically for clarity and flow
- **references/interview_questions.md**: Structured interview protocol for gathering context and a refinement diagnostic checklist

## Core Principles

1. The literature review is purely conceptual and theoretical; measurement methodology and analytical procedures belong in the methods section
2. Build a progressive argument: theory exposition, prior research synthesis, limitation identification, new approach motivation, earned research questions
3. Dense citation support with specific study findings woven into narrative prose, not cataloged
4. Two subsections with descriptive headers naming the dimension or activity
5. Each paragraph has one clear through-line advancing the argument
6. Research questions emerge as the logical conclusion of the argument, not as restatements of the introduction
7. No bullet points, no em-dashes, no italicized sublabels within paragraphs

## Argumentative Architecture

The literature review traces a progressive argument across two subsections. The templates (in references/) describe what goes where; this section describes why each element appears where it does.

### Subsection 1 (Theoretical Framework and Domain Application)

Role: Establish the theoretical framework in full, trace its extension to the study's domain, and close with the gap.

- P1 establishes the framework (core constructs, key evidence, mechanisms)
- P2 shows it applies beyond its original context (digital, mediated, computational extensions)
- P3 shows the study's domain needs it (what existing frameworks capture and miss)
- P4 shows where it has not yet been applied (the specific understudied context)

Each paragraph opens by extending or complicating the prior paragraph's conclusion.

### Subsection 2 (Prior Research Tradition to New Approach)

Role: Review the field's research tradition, show how the environment restructures the phenomenon, identify methodological limitations, and arrive at the study's approach.

- P5 establishes what the field knows and how it knows it (foundational studies, methodological pattern)
- P6 shows the environment has changed (platform dynamics, content diversity, new metrics)
- P7 shows existing tools cannot capture the new reality (instrument and analytical limitations)
- P8 introduces the approach that can (content-side framing, earned RQs)

### Subsection Headers

Use descriptive headers naming the dimension or activity:
- "[Theory] in [Domain]: [Construct 1] and [Construct 2] in [Context]"
- "[Field]-Mediated [Activity]: From [Existing Approach] to [New Approach]"

### Research Question Framing

RQs should feel earned by the argument. Use framing that connects each question to the preceding analysis rather than restating the introduction's wording (e.g., "The foregoing analysis motivates [N] questions. First, if [construct] is a property of [unit], how is it distributed...").

## Workflow

### Phase 1: Context Interview

Conduct a structured interview using **references/interview_questions.md**. If an introduction already exists, read it first to identify what literature it previews (these studies should be elaborated, not re-introduced).

### Phase 2: Outline

Map the progressive argument across 8 paragraphs (4 per subsection). For each paragraph, identify the through-line and key citations. Verify the argument arrives at the RQs as a natural conclusion.

### Phase 3: Draft

Draft following the template in **references/literature_review_template.md**. Write flowing paragraphs of 6-10 sentences each. Lead with substance, not meta-commentary.

### Phase 4: Cross-Section Coordination Check

If an introduction exists, verify:
- [ ] The intro compresses its literature treatment; the lit review elaborates
- [ ] Theory is defined in full in the lit review; the intro references it briefly
- [ ] Concrete illustrative examples appear in the lit review, not the intro
- [ ] Coined phrases appear in one section only
- [ ] RQ framing differs between the two sections

### Phase 5: Refinement

When the user has an existing draft, use the refinement diagnostic in **references/interview_questions.md** (final section) to identify structural issues, then revise following the argumentative architecture above. Apply the quality checklist.

## Scope

### Include in the literature review
- Theoretical framework: origins, dimensions, mechanisms, extensions, domain applications
- Synthesis of prior research findings with specific study contributions
- Gaps emerging as natural consequences of the narrative trajectory
- Conceptual motivation for the study's analytical approach
- Research questions as the earned conclusion

### Reserve for other sections
- Measurement methodology and operationalization (methods)
- Analytical procedures, model specification, technical implementation (methods)
- Empirical results and findings (results)
- Theoretical interpretation and implications (discussion)

## Quality Checklist

- [ ] Purely conceptual and theoretical (no measurement methodology or analytical procedures)
- [ ] Progressive argument traces theory, application, limitations, new approach, RQs
- [ ] Two subsections with descriptive headers
- [ ] Each paragraph has one clear through-line
- [ ] Specific study findings woven into narrative (not cataloged)
- [ ] Dense citation support throughout
- [ ] Research questions feel earned by the argument (not restated from intro)
- [ ] No bullet points, no em-dashes, no italicized sublabels in prose
- [ ] No redundant sentences (each sentence advances the argument)
- [ ] No standalone intro paragraphs that merely announce what follows
- [ ] Cross-section coordination verified (if intro exists)
- [ ] Appropriate length for venue
